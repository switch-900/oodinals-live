Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 23, 2026

Overview

Oodinals is a client-side Bitcoin Ordinals inscription and trading application. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data we collect, what we don't, and how the platform operates.

What We Do Not Collect

  • No accounts or registration. Oodinals does not require you to create an account, provide an email address, or sign up.
  • No private keys. Your wallet keys never leave your device. All transaction signing happens locally in your browser or wallet extension.
  • No tracking cookies. We do not use advertising trackers, analytics cookies, or third-party tracking scripts.
  • No personal data storage. We do not store names, emails, IP addresses, or other personally identifiable information on our servers.

What Happens Locally

Oodinals runs entirely in your browser. The following data may be stored in your browser's local storage for your convenience:

  • Your selected ordinals origin preference
  • Wallet connection state (public address only)
  • UI preferences (theme, settings)

You can clear this data at any time by clearing your browser's local storage for this site.

Blockchain Interactions

When you create inscriptions or execute trades, transactions are broadcast to the Bitcoin network. Bitcoin transactions are public and permanently recorded on the blockchain. This is inherent to Bitcoin, not specific to Oodinals.

Third-Party Services

Oodinals may communicate with the following external services to function:

  • ordinals.com — for loading inscribed code dependencies and content from the Bitcoin blockchain
  • Bitcoin mempool APIs — for fee rate estimation and transaction broadcasting
  • Wallet extensions (e.g., Xverse, Unisat, Bitmap Wallet) — these are third-party browser extensions governed by their own privacy policies

Open Source

Oodinals is open source. You can inspect the code to verify these claims. No data leaves your browser except Bitcoin transactions you explicitly sign and broadcast.

Education & Resources

To learn more about Bitcoin Ordinals, visit 0rdinals.com — the canonical resource for the Ordinals protocol, inscription theory, and sat numbering.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy, reach out via the project's GitHub repository or community channels.
